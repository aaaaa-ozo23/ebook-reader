## Reader UI follow-up

- TXT, EPUB-derived readers (including MOBI/AZW3), and PDF share `ReaderPageButton`, which renders a 20 px SVG chevron outside the Previous/Next label at every viewport.
- Reading Settings uses explicit `line`, `spacing`, and `margin` typography variants. Their icons express baseline distance, paragraph separation, and text-column inset instead of reusing one glyph.
- Runtime geometry tests prove narrow/medium/wide margins produce distinct content widths or gutters for TXT, EPUB, and PDF. TXT and EPUB preserve reading anchors during reflow; PDF keeps custom zoom while changing stage space. The medium setting is persisted and verified after reopen.
- The Bookmarks empty state now matches the approved dark-sidebar board: an outline bookmark icon, concise title, explanatory copy, and a transparent full-width `Add bookmark` row separated from the content. The redundant sidebar `Add note` action is removed; notes continue to originate from an actual text selection and remain available in the Notes list.
- EPUB-derived formats and PDF expose both requested and rendered page view in the runtime DOM. Requested Double expands the EPUB reading column before the adapter measures it, and the desktop threshold is 820 px for both adapters. Playwright verifies EPUB/MOBI/AZW3 and PDF render Double with reader chrome visible while 640/375 px layouts continue to fall back to Single without overwriting the saved preference.
